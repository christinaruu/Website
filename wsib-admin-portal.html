WSIB Lab - Admin Portal

Overview – Project portal for administration

Role – Designer along with 1 other designer and 4 developers

Tools – Sketch

Context

The Lab is constantly working on a variety of different projects, whether it be for research or the corporate team. Currently, only the website is available for the public to view project updates. It was brought to our attention to develop a portal for the corporate admins to view details of all our projects, more in depth.

Goal

To design a way for admin to view updates and details of ongoing and archived projects

Target Users

WSIB Administration

User Flow and Wireframes

The main dashboard would display preview cards of all the projects categorized into ‘Active’ and ‘Archived’ sections. Within each preview card, there is a feature that allows the user to download a slide deck containing information about the project. Once a preview card is chosen, a more detailed page of the project appears.

The most important details we wanted to emphasize were:

  • For active project, what stage a project was currently at and its percentage of completion
  • For archived projects, whether a project was successfully completed or not
  • What the learning goals for the Lab were

Initial Designs

The initial versions tried to emphasize the new chameleon logo with a new corresponding colour palette. The general feedback was that the design had too many warm tones and gave off a ‘forest’ feel.


Based on previous feedback, we decided to aim for feelings of innovation, technology, and modernism instead. The general feedback was that the design felt cold and stiff, and the triangle designs gave off an aggressive feeling.

Final Design

The final design incorporates the use of more graphics and both warm and cool toned colours. The goal was to create a warm but also modern and professional feeling. The graphic in the login page was to indicate the context of the portal; that the Lab is actively contributing together to work through projects.

Results

The general feedback of the final design was positive and encouraging. The design and development has been completed and the product is currently waiting on more input.
